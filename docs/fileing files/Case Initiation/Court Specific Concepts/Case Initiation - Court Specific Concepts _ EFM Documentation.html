COVID-19 Question for New Unlawful Detainer Cases

Unlawful Detainer is defined as cases with the following case categories:
  • Unlawful Detainer - Drugs
  • Unlawful Detainer - Post Foreclosure
  • Unlawful Detainer/Commercial (not drugs or wrongful eviction)
  • Unlawful Detainer/Residential (not drugs or wrongful eviction)
  • Small Claims

LASC requires all EFSPs to display the following question to end-users when filing a new Unlawful Detainer case:

Is this an Action for Recovery of COVID-19 Related Debt as defined under Section 1179.02?

If the answer is YES, your ReviewFiling must contain the following snippet: 

<ns6:CaseSpecialStatus>
  <ns6:SpecialStatusItem>
    <ns6:statusCode>UDCOV19</ns6:statusCode>
  </ns6:SpecialStatusItem>
</ns6:CaseSpecialStatus>

Complex Case Augmentations

There are two additional Booleans for complex cases that can be used during case initiations and are dependent upon the Complex Indicator and are not able to be submitted without it. There will be validations enforcing this.
Class Action

This checkbox can be used on case initiation to mark a complex case as a Class Action Lawsuit and must contain the following case augmentation in XML. 

   </a:CaseAugmentation>
   <ns5:AmountInControversy xsi:type="ns4:AmountType">0</ns5:AmountInControversy>
   <ns5:ClassActionIndicator>true</ns5:ClassActionIndicator>
   <ns5:JurisdictionalGroundsCode>O25K</ns5:JurisdictionalGroundsCode>
   <ns6:CaseTypeText>CU</ns6:CaseTypeText>
   <ns6:CaseAugmentationExt>
   <ns3:CaseCourt>
Asbestos

This is used in conjunction with the specific case Category of "Asbestos- Personal Injury/Wrongful Death" when creating a new complex case. This assigns a special case status in EFM and may be required in the future. 

   </ns6:CaseAugmentationExt>
   <ns6:complexLitigation>true</ns6:complexLitigation>
   <ns6:asbestos>true</ns6:asbestos>

Nevada Superior Court

Consent to eService

Per California Rule 2.251 , a party or other person may indicate consent to accept electronic service and distribution of documents. This consent includes documents generated from the Court such as Judgments and Orders.

Language suggestion:

Consent to eService

eService is mandated for all eFiling parties pursuant to Local Rule 1.06. You will receive electronic service of notices and documents related to this case. PLEASE NOTE: The email address provided to the court at the time of filing will be used for electronic service of documents.

Case Initiation
During case initiation, Nevada Superior Court expects the eService consent element below to be added to either:

  • Self-represented parties
  • Attorneys

<p:eService>true</p:eService>

Placer Superior Court

Consent to eService

Per California Rule 2.251 , a party or other person may indicate consent to accept electronic service and distribution of documents. This consent includes documents generated from the Court such as Judgments and Orders.

The rule DOES NOT prescribe specific language for a provision of a term of service when the filer consents to electronic service, but does require that any such provision be clear. Consent to Electronic Service and Notice of Electronic Service Address (form EFS-005-CV) provides an example of language for consenting to electronic service.

The below language is now required by Placer, the notice needs to read something similar to this:

On behalf of myself/my client I consent to receiving electronic service from the Court (This is not indicating consent to electronic service from other parties on the case.)

Case Initiation
During case initiation, Placer expects the eService consent element below to be added to either:

  • self-rep parties
  • attorneys

<p:eService>true</p:eService>

Please review the following scenarios in the Placer County Review Filing Examples :

  • New Case with Consent to eService Self Represented
  • New Case with Consent to eService by Filing Attorney

Sacramento Superior Court

ParentLocationCode Requirement

Sacramento Superior Court will not be using Zip Codes with a directory location assigned to them. Instead, EFSPs should be using the following call on their XML as Sacramento cases are routed based on Case Type and Case Category: 

niem/courtLocations/zipCode/{zipCode}/caseType/{caseType}/caseCategory/{caseCategory}

The 3 parameters (zipCode / caseType / caseCategory) need to be set as optional, so if submitted with a null zipCode, it will still return the correct location.

 

Ventura Superior Court

Minor as AS TO Party

Ventura court requires that Minors be added as the AS TO party for case initiation in the following Case Categories:

  • Petition for Change of Name
  • Petition for Minor’s Change of Gender / Change of Name
New required Address fields for AS TO party have been added to the Case Initiation screen for these Case Categories. The address data needs to be collected and passed through to EFM. In order to file the name/gender petitions (with fee waivers) for minors, parents will need to be added as the FILED BY party with fee waivers and the Minor as the AS TO (refers to) party.

Small Claims

Ventura court requires an Address for the AS TO party on case initiation of Small Claims cases. The address data needs to be collected and passed through to EFM. New required Address fields for AS TO party have been added to the Case Initiation screen for Small Claims case type.
